# Naviga Life copy voice

## Voice

Write with calm clinical confidence and human warmth. Name the device, what it does, and the conditions it addresses in plain language. Prefer specific, short sentences over claims of superiority. Explain practical home or clinical use without sounding like an advertisement.

Product descriptions should usually answer four questions in this order:

1. What is the device?
2. Which conditions or risks does it address?
3. How does the treatment work in practical terms?
4. Which controls, garments, or use settings distinguish this model?

Keep claims factual. The owner has directed that the source medical claims may be treated as verified, but the copy must not promise a cure or guaranteed outcome.

## Style rules

- Use active voice and sentence case.
- Keep body sentences compact and paragraphs under 70 words.
- Expand a clinical abbreviation on first use, such as chronic venous insufficiency (CVI) or deep vein thrombosis (DVT).
- Use “supports,” “helps manage,” “designed for,” and “intended to reduce risk” when they are accurate.
- Name lymphedema, DVT prevention, varicose veins, venous ulcers, chronic venous insufficiency, diabetic foot and amputation prevention, cellulitis, peripheral arterial disease, and filariasis where relevant.
- Preserve meaningful model names. Lightly clean spacing and capitalization.
- Keep specifications factual, concise, and consistently capitalized.
- Use numerals with units: `240 mmHg`, `45 minutes`, `6 chambers`.
- Use hyphens for simple compound modifiers. Avoid strings of parenthetical claims.
- Never use exclamation marks or em-dash chains.

## Banned or discouraged language

Do not use: revolutionary, cutting-edge, game-changing, miracle, cure, guaranteed, best-in-class, world-class, ultimate, flawless, unmatched, unbeatable, advanced technology without explaining the feature, 100% safe, 100% effective, pain-free, instant results, transform your life, or clinically proven unless a named study is supplied.

Do not use generic filler such as “designed with you in mind,” “innovative solution,” “premium quality,” or “state-of-the-art.” Replace it with the concrete chamber count, pressure range, garment configuration, cycle control, or intended condition.

## Product example

Before, intentionally off-voice:

> Our revolutionary compression machine is a cutting-edge solution that delivers amazing results for every patient. It is easy to use and offers premium quality treatment at home.

After:

> The KL 3000 Pro Neo provides sequential pneumatic compression through a multi-chamber leg garment. It supports home treatment for lymphedema and chronic venous insufficiency and may help reduce the risk of complications associated with poor lower-limb circulation. Adjustable pressure and timed cycles let a clinician tailor the session to the prescribed plan.

## Testimonial example

Placeholder testimonial:

> “The leg sleeve took a few sessions to position quickly, but the controls were straightforward once my therapist showed me. I now use the pump during my evening reading, and the swelling from lymphedema feels easier to manage.”
>
> Meera S., Pune · `placeholder: true`

Testimonials must sound individual, moderately positive, and condition-specific. Vary length and rhythm. Include one credible limitation across the set, such as pump noise, cuff setup, or the time needed for a session. Never invent a clinician endorsement or guaranteed medical result.

## Required disclaimer

Use this sentence unchanged in the public site and catalogue PDFs:

> Individual results may vary. Please consult your physician for guidance specific to your condition.
